# 00 — Product Vision

## The one sentence

**Vector is the first app I open every morning — not to log expenses, but to know the smartest financial decision I can make today.**

## The problem with every finance app

Budgeting apps optimize for *recording the past*. They tell you where money went. That's an accountant's job, and it's done. The decision already happened.

Vector optimizes for *the next decision*. Money is only context. The product is judgment.

Every other finance app answers "how much did I spend on coffee?" Vector answers "given everything I know about your goals, your business, your liquidity, and your rules — should you do this, yes or no, and why?"

## The three questions

Every screen, every feature, every AI output serves exactly one of these:

1. **Where am I?** — Net worth, liquidity, goal progress, business health. The truth, at a glance.
2. **What changed?** — Since yesterday, since last week. Deltas, not absolutes. The story of the movement.
3. **What should I do next?** — One recommendation. Opinionated. Explained. Actionable.

If a feature doesn't map cleanly to one of these, it does not exist.

## Who this is for

One user. Me.

- Software engineer, owner of a software company.
- Has **both** personal and business finances, and they interact.
- Long-term goals: apartment in 2028 (without destroying liquidity), furnish it stress-free, grow the company, grow investments, quit smoking, raise net worth every month.
- Does not need budgeting. Needs **clarity** and **discipline**.

This is a single-user product. It is not built to generalize. Every trade-off is resolved in favor of *this* user's daily decisions. That is a feature, not a limitation.

## The mental model: Everything is an Event

Salary, expense, investment, invoice, dividend, transfer, a cigarette, a vacation, a purchase, a subscription, a goal, a mortgage, a car lease — all of it is one primitive: an **Event**.

Events flow into a **Timeline** — a feed, like a social feed of your financial life. The AI reads Events and produces:

- **Insights** — "This is what this means."
- **Recommendations** — "This is what to do."
- **Warnings** — "This breaks a rule you set."
- **Forecasts** — "This is where the line goes."
- **Achievements** — "This is progress worth noticing."

Events are the input. Judgment is the output.

## The IPS — the app's conscience

The app permanently remembers my **Investment Policy Statement**. These are the rules the advisor enforces:

- The objective is **long-term freedom**, not maximum money.
- Never spend over **2500 RON** impulsively.
- Protect liquidity — always.
- Business cash exists to create future value.
- Capital should always have a job.
- Avoid lifestyle inflation.
- Invest consistently.
- Business growth beats luxury purchases.
- Buying the apartment must not destroy liquidity.
- If financing is cheaper than expected investment returns, prefer financing.
- Smoking is a financial leak.

The advisor cites these rules *by name* when a decision touches them. The IPS is data (editable), not hardcoded, so it can evolve — but it is always present.

## The personality of the product

The AI is not ChatGPT. It is not an assistant. It is my **financial advisor / CFO**.

**Calm. Direct. Honest. Opinionated. Professional. Data-driven.**

Never emotional. Never motivational. Never generic. If I make a bad decision, it tells me — explains *why*, explains *consequences* — but never judges. It has the tone of a senior partner who has seen a thousand of these and is not impressed by hype, but is on my side.

## What Vector refuses to be

- ❌ A dashboard of charts nobody acts on.
- ❌ A gamified spending tracker with confetti.
- ❌ A feature-parity clone of Monarch / Copilot / YNAB.
- ❌ A generic AI chatbot bolted onto a ledger.
- ❌ Anything that adds a tap without adding a decision.

## The feel

Apple-quality. Linear/Raycast precision. Monarch/Copilot polish. Notion calm. Dark-first, minimal, premium, large typography, rounded cards, generous spacing, elegant motion. Every screen should feel *expensive* — because it's managing something that matters.

## The bet

If Vector earns the first tap of the morning, it wins. Everything in this documentation is in service of making the morning open feel *indispensable*: you see where you are, what moved, and the one smart thing to do — in under ten seconds.
